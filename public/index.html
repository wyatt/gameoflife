Conway's Game of Life

This was made for a small project in order to learn the basics of Phaser. It displays an example of a zero player game, known as the Game of Life, devised by John Conway in 1970. It is called a 'cellular automata' and it simulates population growth and decline. There are many initial patterns that determine the result of the game, each with their own unique properties. This particular pattern is known as the Gosper glider gun and is the first infinitely growing start pattern discovered.
